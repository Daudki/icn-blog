Overview

Troubleshooting is a structured way to isolate faults in networks. Good engineers test assumptions, check layers, and document evidence as they go.

Projects

Practice the core steps of finding and explaining a network issue.

Key Takeaways

Check layers

Many problems are easier to solve when you test from the physical layer upward.

Document findings

Good notes make repeated incidents much easier to resolve.

Verify fixes

Always confirm the issue is truly resolved after making a change.
